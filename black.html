Black Pepper Farming for Profit

Learn to cultivate the "King of Spices" sustainably and profitably

📅 Best Season to Plant

Black pepper is best planted during the **monsoon months (May–June)**. It thrives in hot, humid tropical climates with **1500–2500 mm of rainfall** annually.

🌱 How to Farm Black Pepper

  • Choose fertile, well-drained soil with a pH of 5.5–6.5.
  • Plant cuttings near live support trees (like silver oak or coconut).
  • Provide partial shade and keep soil moist but not waterlogged.
  • Apply organic compost and mulch regularly to retain moisture.
  • Support vines with proper staking and pruning for airflow.

💡 Profit Strategies

  • Sell dried black pepper directly to spice traders and markets.
  • Package under your own organic or gourmet spice brand.
  • Export to Europe, Middle East, and premium markets.
  • Start intercropping with cardamom, turmeric, or coffee.
  • Sell value-added products like ground pepper or pepper oil.

📈 Income Potential

Black pepper yields start from the **third year** and increase yearly. An acre can produce **400–600 kg of dried pepper**, and market prices range from ₹500–₹800 per kg, depending on quality and grade.

🌿 Expert Tips

  • Use disease-resistant varieties like Panniyur-1, Karimunda.
  • Protect against foot rot and leaf spot using bio-fungicides.
  • Ensure good drainage and avoid stagnant water.
  • Harvest when berries turn red, and dry in sunlight for 3–4 days.
