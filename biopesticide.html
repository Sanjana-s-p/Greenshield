What is a Bio-Pesticide?

Bio-pesticides are derived from natural materials like animals, plants, bacteria, and certain minerals. They are used to manage pests in an environmentally friendly and sustainable way. Unlike chemical pesticides, bio-pesticides are safer for humans, beneficial insects, and the ecosystem.

Importance of Bio-Pesticides

  • Reduces chemical residues on food crops.
  • Preserves biodiversity and soil health.
  • Minimizes resistance development in pests.
  • Enhances long-term pest control strategies.
  • Safe for pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Benefits of Using Bio-Pesticides

  • Target-specific, reducing harm to non-target species.
  • Biodegradable and leave no toxic residue.
  • Compliant with organic farming standards.
  • Lower environmental impact than chemical alternatives.
  • Can be integrated into IPM (Integrated Pest Management) systems.

Quick Facts

🔬 Bio-pesticides can be microbial (bacteria, fungi, viruses), biochemical (plant extracts, pheromones), or plant-incorporated protectants (PIPs).

✅ Approved bio-pesticides include *Bacillus thuringiensis* (Bt), neem oil, and Trichoderma fungi.
