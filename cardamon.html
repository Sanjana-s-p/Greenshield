Cardamom Farming for Profit

Grow the Queen of Spices – sustainably and profitably

📆 Best Season to Plant

Cardamom is best planted during the **monsoon season (June to August)**. It thrives in **humid climates**, at altitudes between **600–1500 meters**, with **ample shade and 1500–4000 mm rainfall** annually.

🌱 How to Farm Cardamom

  • Select loamy, fertile, well-drained soil with good organic content.
  • Maintain pH between 6.0–6.8.
  • Plant cardamom rhizomes in pits (30cm x 30cm) spaced 2 ft apart.
  • Provide partial to full shade using natural canopy or shade trees.
  • Regularly mulch and irrigate during dry spells.

💡 Profit Ideas

  • Sell dried green cardamom pods to spice traders and exporters.
  • Package for retail under your own organic spice brand.
  • Export to international markets (Middle East, Europe, US).
  • Create byproducts like cardamom oil and herbal infusions.
  • Offer agro-tourism or cardamom garden experiences.

📈 Profit Potential

Yields can range from **100–300 kg per acre**, with prices between **₹1500–₹3000/kg**, depending on quality. That’s ₹2–6+ lakhs per acre annually.

🌿 Farming Tips

  • Use improved varieties like Njallani, PV-1, and CCS-1 for higher yield.
  • Control pests like thrips and root grubs using bio-pesticides.
  • Hand-harvest pods at 80% maturity and dry them slowly in shade.
  • Rotate crops after 4–5 years to maintain soil fertility.
